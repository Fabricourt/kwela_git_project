Learn More About Lamu & The Lapsett

Lamu county occupies an area of 6,273.1 km² has a population of 101,539. The main settlers of lamu county are the Boni somali , mijikenda and Kikuyus.

The main economical activities is fishing and tourism farming is now also becoming a serious income earner including livestock

Lamu is the leading producer of cotton in Kenya , Trees also do very well. Fruit trees like cashew nuts, mango coconuts.

Most of the land is unoccupied due to the low density in population. However it is expected, within the ongoing construction of the port and other lapsett projects , the population will grow and with it the need to produce move food to feed the people.

In 1975 the Kenya goverment decided to construct a new port in lamu to complement the one in mombasa.

Lamu was choosen as a result of the sutability of lamu which has a deep natural harbour of 18m. This would see all the ocean faring vessels which cannot dock in Mombasa because of its depth docking in Lamu.

It was not until 2012 that the goverment of Kenya took the 1st step toward its realisation. IT saw the president of South Sudan and the prime minister of Ethiopia putting a commumeration stone at the place where the port would be constructed.

The whole project which is reffered to as the lapsset is expected to cost ksh3.2 trion it has the following components.

  1. Lapsset Roads

    Lamu south sudan Ethiopia transport corridor (Lapsset)

    road going all the way to juba in south sudan the other branching to Ethiopia from Isiolo throught moyale.

  2. Oil pipelie from south sudan through turkana to lamu

  3. Oil Refinery

  4. Coal find plant at Lamu

  5. Resort cities at L.Turkana Isiolo and lamu

  6. Two international airports at Lamu and Isiolo.

  7. Railway line linking juba Ethiopia and Nairobi from Lamu
